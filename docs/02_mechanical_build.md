# Mechanical build

## Early hotplate version
- 1500 W cooking hotplate used as the starting point
- 2 mm aluminum top plate added above it
- PCB holder mounted on top
- laser thermometer used to find a repeatable preheat position on the mechanical knob

## Base
- 50 x 50 cm aluminum base plate
- 10 mm thick
- drilled and tapped for the frame and rails

## Frame and motion
- T-slot profiles cut to length with a miter saw
- holes measured, drilled, and tapped
- 20 x 40 profiles used for the main motion structure
- Y-axis initially too tight
- inner wheels were removed so the motion became smoother
- final system supports movement and plate rotation

## Microscope support
- center-mounted support built from T-slot extrusion
- designed to keep the microscope aligned over the work area

## Armature / hand rest
- designed as a foldable part in CAD from 2 mm aluminum sheet
- bend grooves were added to make forming easier
- toolpaths and G-code were generated in Kiri:Moto
- cut on a DIY CNC with plywood sacrificial board, double-sided tape, and clamps
- bent with a simple wood jig
- mounted with aluminum rivets and spacers to keep a cooler air gap from the hot zone

## Control box mount
- custom aluminum bracket connects the control box to the moving plate assembly
- screen and controls stay near the work area
- cable pull is reduced during movement
